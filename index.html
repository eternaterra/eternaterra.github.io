001 — Mission

Growing the impossible,
beautifully.

Eternaterra is a design and research studio that creates controlled environment agriculture facilities for places where conventional farming fails. We combine architecture, environmental engineering, and cultivation science to grow extraordinary crops in extraordinary conditions.

Our first station is an 8,000 sq ft timber-frame barn in upstate New York — retrofitted into a multi-biome growing facility that produces tropical, exotic, and high-value crops through zone 5b winters. Vanilla. Saffron. Wasabi. Cacao. Things that have no business growing in the snow belt.

If it grows here, it grows anywhere on Earth. Or off it.

Every place on Earth has the potential to grow food. The question isn't whether — it's how beautifully you can design the system that makes it possible.

003 — Stations

One barn. Then the world.

Each Eternaterra station is a controlled environment facility designed for a specific impossible condition. The barn is first. The rest follow the question: where else can we grow?

Station One sits on a farm in upstate New York — surrounded by deer, hills, and some of the harshest winters in the Northeast. If the system works here, through a zone 5b February, it works anywhere. That's the point.

Upstate New York

8,000 sq ft timber-frame barn. Zone 5b winters. Multi-biome tropical, arid, mushroom, vertical, and research zones.
